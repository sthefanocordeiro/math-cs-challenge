# Self-Taught Curriculum: Pure Mathematics & Theoretical Computer Science

This repository documents my 12-month personal challenge to master the foundations of **Pure Mathematics** and **Theoretical Computer Science** using exclusively open and free resources. 

The goal is to move beyond applied programming and shallow tutorials, focusing instead on mathematical rigor, formal proofs, and the theoretical underpinnings of computation.

## 🎯 The Challenge
- **Duration:** 52 weeks (April 2026 – April 2027)
- **Commitment:** ~8 hours per week (~416 hours total)
- **Philosophy:** Focus on canonical textbooks, proof-based learning, and formalization in Lean/Coq.
- **Constraints:** No paid courses, no "black-box" libraries, and no surface-level MOOCs.

## 📚 Curriculum Overview

The study plan is divided into four main pillars, designed to provide a deep understanding of both abstract structures and computational limits:

1. **Foundations & Analysis:** Transitioning from calculus to rigorous Real Analysis (using Abel's and Lebl's frameworks).
2. **Abstract Structures:** Exploration of Group Theory, Rings, and Topology (Munkres).
3. **Theory of Computation:** Formal languages, automata, and complexity theory (Sipser).
4. **Formal Logic & Verification:** Hands-on experience with interactive theorem provers like Lean 4 and Coq/Rocq.

## 🛠 Methodology
Instead of passive video consumption, this challenge prioritizes:
- **Direct Reading:** Engaging with classic academic literature.
- **Problem Sets:** Solving end-of-chapter exercises to ensure conceptual mastery.
- **Formalization:** Writing proofs that can be verified by a computer to ensure 100% correctness.
- **Open Tracking:** All progress, notes, and solved exercises will be pushed to this repository.

## 📂 Repository Structure
- `/plan`: Detailed 52-week study schedule.
- `/notes`: Personal summaries and LaTeX notes on core concepts.
- `/proofs`: Formalization scripts (Lean/Coq).
- `/solutions`: Selected solutions to textbook exercises.
